**Open Response Assessment (ORA)** lets learners submit free text (and optionally a file). Grading is rubric-based — by a peer, by an instructor, or both. This is the heaviest tool in the box; use it deliberately.

## Three grading workflows

| Workflow | How it works | Right for |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-assessment** | Learner grades their own work against the rubric | Reflective writing |
| **Peer assessment** | Other learners grade anonymously | Cohort programmes with critical mass |
| **Staff grading** | Course staff grade in the LMS | Anything that affects CPD certification |

For most Liverpool Dental CPD modules, **self-assessment is the right default** — peer grading needs more enrolled-at-once learners than self-paced CPD typically has.

## Designing a good rubric

Three to five criteria, each with three to five levels (e.g. *Not yet — Developing — Proficient*). Write level descriptors that name observable behaviours, not vague quality words.

Bad: *Excellent answer*
Good: *Identifies at least three differential diagnoses and justifies each with reference to clinical findings*

## Configuration tips

- **Word count** — set a minimum to discourage one-line answers, but no maximum.
- **File upload** — allow PDFs for case write-ups; allow images for radiograph annotation submissions.
- **Submission deadline** — use a wide window for self-paced courses (months, not days).

## Caveats

- Peer assessment requires a quorum (default 3 peers). It can stall in low-enrolment courses.
- Self-assessment is honest but not auditable for certification — consider pairing with a graded MCQ for the CPD-hours-claim record.
